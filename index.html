Project Idea
Overview

My idea for a project is an app for managing ‘Secret Santa’s’. It would be a simple way for users to enter into a Secret Santa group, set a price limit, have a wish list, randomly allocate a ‘buyer’ to a ‘receiver’ and allow them to anonymously communicate for clarifications e.g. What colour would you prefer for item 3 on your list? Or Could you please specify what type of headphones you would like? The app would be very simple in terms of usability and would work for families, friends or in a workplace.

Motivation

The motivation behind this project is that often secret santa’s involve one person that has to organise and coordinate the different aspects of a secret santa. For example, normally someone involved in the secret santa will choose the names out of a hat (or some other method) and then tells each person who they are buying a present for and give them that person's wish list. This means that there will always be one person in the group that knows who everyones Secret Santa is. Another reason is that most secret santa apps simply randomise the names and offer no other real service. There are websites that offer these some of these services however most people would want to access their secret santa information on their phone. Eg looking at someone's wish list while shopping. An app would be a simple solution to this.

Description

The app would have 4 main functions including allocation, wish lists, groups and communication.

The group function of the app would allow for people to create or join a Secret Santa group and be apart of multiple secret santa groups at once. For example, one person could be apart of a Secret Santa group with their friends and another group with their colleagues and this function would allow them to have separate group members, wish lists and spending limits. One person will create a group and invite people to join the group through an email. The person who created the group will set spending limit e.g. $50. Which could then be approved by other members of the group as they join.

The allocation aspect of the app would be a simple randomization tool that randomly assigns each user another user to be a Secret Santa for, while prohibiting each user from receiving their own name as their secret santa.

The app would also have an option to include wishlists. Wishlists would be a list of 3 to 10 items that people would like to receive from their secret santa as well as the option to include rough prices for each item. For example:

User ‘A’ Wishlist: Book Title by Author Name Price Estimate:$25

These lists would then be accessed by the chosen secret santa who could make notes on each item such as ‘this would be cheaper on amazon’ or ‘difficult to locate’, these notes would not be visible to the maker of the list. The purpose of this function would be to allow the secret santa to choose which item/s they will buy, how much the items cost from various places and compare items.

Once the secret santa knows who they are buying for and have viewed the wishlist, they will also have access to a feature for anonymous communication with who they are buying for.The purpose of the communication function would be to clarify wishlist items or other relevant information. For example, if someone put ‘t-shirts’ on their wishlist the communication tool would allow the secret santa to ask questions such as ‘What size t-shirts?’ or ‘What address should I send your gift to?’ which would mean that the receiver would be more likely to receive what they would like while also not revealing who the secret santa is.
